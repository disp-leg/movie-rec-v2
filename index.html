Right at Your Door

2006 · Chris Gorak · 6.0

A dirty bomb goes off in LA and a husband seals himself inside his home with plastic sheeting and duct tape, shutting his wife out. The confined-space paranoia and the gut-punch twist ending share The Divide's unflinching bleakness about what people do to survive. The entire film takes place in and around one house, and the domestic setting makes the horror feel unbearably intimate -- much like The Divide's basement bunker. The ending reframes everything and leaves you hollowed out.

“One of the best post-9/11 anxiety films. The ending is devastating in a way that recontextualizes the entire movie.”
